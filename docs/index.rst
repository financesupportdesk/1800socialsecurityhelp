     <h2>What Is the $1,800 Social Security Increase?</h2>
     <p>
       The <strong>$1,800 Social Security increase</strong> in 2026 is a potential monthly benefit boost linked to the Cost-of-Living Adjustment (COLA). This increase is designed to help seniors manage rising living costs. However, the actual amount may vary based on your eligibility and current benefits.
     </p>

     <h2>Who Is Eligible?</h2>
     <ul>
       <li>You are already receiving <strong>Social Security Retirement, SSI, or SSDI benefits</strong></li>
       <li>You have reached or are near <strong>full retirement age</strong></li>
       <li>You are a <strong>U.S. citizen or permanent resident</strong></li>
       <li>You meet <strong>federal or state program requirements</strong></li>
     </ul>

     <h2>What You Will Need</h2>
     <ul>
       <li>Your <strong>Social Security Number (SSN)</strong></li>
       <li>Proof of income or benefit statement</li>
       <li><strong>Government-issued ID</strong></li>
       <li>Bank details for <strong>direct deposit</strong></li>
     </ul>

     <h2>Step-by-Step: How to Apply</h2>
     <ol>
       <li>Visit the official Social Security website.</li>
       <li>Log in to your <strong>mySocialSecurity account</strong> or create a new one.</li>
       <li>Go to the <strong>“Benefit Updates”</strong> or 2026 increase section.</li>
       <li>Fill out the online application form with accurate details.</li>
       <li>Submit your application and wait for confirmation.</li>
     </ol>

     <h2>How Long Does It Take?</h2>
